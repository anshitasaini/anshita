About Me

I’m Anshita Saini, a rising senior at Interlake High School in Bellevue, WA. Since the age of five, I’ve been immersed in art as a learner of both Hindustani Classical Music and Kathak, a form of Indian classical dance, as well as the flute. I'm also passionate about aeronautics and astronautics, which I've explored through model rocketry and astrophysics research with the Summer Science Program. Through my work as the Founder and President of Code4Care, Co-Founder of InSync, and Vice President of She Codes Art, I’ve had the privilege of impacting thousands of students by teaching STEM, singing, and dancing, while fundraising for underprivileged populations. Seeing students that once felt intimidated and often bored by the idea of STEM becoming enthusiastic about the projects we teach motivates me to continue extending my impact to nations around the world. Beyond direct impact through my teaching, I’m a researcher at the University of Washington’s Ubiquitous Computing Lab where I’m currently working on detecting stroke symptoms simply by analyzing a recording of a person’s face, and where I've previously co-authored a research paper on quantifying upper-body activities through sonar sensing. These projects will make healthcare more accessible, influencing thousands of people.
